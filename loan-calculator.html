Amortized Loan Calculator

How Amortized Loan Calculator Works

This calculator estimates your fixed monthly installments based on the loan amount, interest rate, and term. Each payment includes both principal and interest, ensuring that the loan is fully repaid by the end of the term.

Advantages

  • Easy to understand with fixed monthly payments
  • Builds equity over time as principal reduces
  • Predictable repayment schedule
  • Suitable for long-term planning

Limitations

  • Interest can be high in early years
  • Less flexible if you want to pay off early
  • Doesn’t cover extra fees like penalties or charges

Deferred Payment Loan Calculator

How Deferred Payment Loan Calculator Works

This calculator shows the total repayment when payments are postponed for a certain period. During deferment, the loan may still accrue interest, which gets added to the principal. At the end of the term, you may need to pay a lump sum or higher installments.

Advantages

  • Helpful for students, startups, or borrowers who need breathing space before repayment
  • Allows you to manage cash flow during the deferment period
  • Simple to calculate total amount due after deferment

Limitations

  • Interest keeps adding during deferment, making the loan more expensive
  • Big lump sum repayment can be difficult
  • Limited eligibility depending on loan type

Bond Loan Calculator

How Bond Loan Calculator Works

This calculator helps you determine the present value of a bond loan by discounting future coupon payments and the face value at maturity. It uses the market interest rate to calculate the fair bond price.

Advantages

  • Useful for investors to evaluate fair value of bonds
  • Helps compare market rate vs coupon rate
  • Shows impact of maturity period and interest rate changes

Limitations

  • Assumes fixed coupon payments (doesn’t cover floating-rate bonds)
  • Market price may still fluctuate due to supply and demand
  • Complex for beginners compared to simple loan calculators

Frequently Asked Questions

Amortized Loan - FAQs

How is the monthly payment calculated?

The monthly payment is calculated using the loan amount, annual interest rate, and loan term. Payments are fixed and each includes both interest and principal so the loan is fully repaid by the end of the term.

Can I pay off the loan early?

Yes — most amortized loans allow early repayment, but check for prepayment penalties or fees. Early payments reduce the principal and therefore future interest.

What happens if my interest rate changes?

If your loan has a fixed rate, monthly payments stay the same. For variable-rate loans, payments may increase or decrease when the rate resets—check your loan terms.

Deferred Payment Loan - FAQs

Does interest accrue during the deferment period?

Often yes. Interest may continue to accrue and be added to the principal, increasing the total amount due after deferment. Some loans offer interest-free deferment—read the terms.

Can I make payments while in deferment?

In many cases you can make voluntary payments during deferment to reduce accrued interest, but it's optional. Contact your lender to confirm permitted actions.

What repayment options exist after deferment ends?

Repayment might be required as a lump sum, higher monthly payments, or an extended term. The exact method depends on the loan agreement.

Bond Loan - FAQs

How is a bond's price determined?

A bond's price is the present value of future coupon payments plus the discounted face value at maturity, using the current market interest rate (yield) as the discount rate.

What affects a bond's yield?

Market interest rates, credit risk of the issuer, time to maturity, and inflation expectations all affect a bond's yield. If market rates rise, existing bond prices usually fall and yields rise.

Can I sell a bond before maturity?

Yes — bonds can be sold in secondary markets. Price on sale depends on current market conditions; selling before maturity may result in gain or loss compared to purchase price.
